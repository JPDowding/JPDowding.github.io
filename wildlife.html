Wildlife

Desert Bighorn Sheep

Desert bighorn sheep are true survivors, adapted to extremes. Cloven hooves give them the grace to skillfully move across rugged terrain, but they can also travel great distances across valleys to reach distant habitat. Learn more>

Mountain Lion

Mountain lions are very rarely seen within the park, and typically avoid the crowded main canyon. However, sightings have been reported in the Kolob Canyons section of Zion, and motion-activated cameras in Zion's backcountry document the presence of these elusive animals. Learn more>

Mule Deer

A common sight in Zion, these graceful creatures can often be seen grazing in the meadows or taking a sip from the Virgin River. They are named for their large mule-like ears. Mule deer tend to have bodies that are a bit larger and heavier than white-tailed deer. Learn more>

Ringtail Cat

A relatively large population of ringtails exists in Zion National Park. The ringtail is related to the raccoon and is completely nocturnal. With big eyes, bigger ears, and a black and white striped tail, they seem like a fairy tale critter. However, they are very real, albeit elusive and extremely smart. Learn more>

Bald Eagle

Winter is an especially good time of year to quietly observe bald eagles, as they migrate into the park from Canada and Alaska for their winter rest. These majestic birds are known for their striking white heads and tails and can be spotted fishing in the park's rivers. Although they were de-listed as endangered species in 2007, bald eagles are still protected under federal law. Learn more>

California Condor

With a wingspan of 9.5 feet and weighing up to 25 pounds, the California condor is the largest land bird in North America. California condors are curious and attracted to human activity. They are frequently seen in Zion perched on or soaring above Angels Landing, and on the Kolob Terrace Road near Lava Point. Learn more>

Mexican Spotted Owl

The Mexican spotted owl ranks as one of the largest owls in North America. The average length 16-19 inches; average wingspan is 42-45 inches; and average weight is 19.5-23 ounces. Mexican spotted owls roost during the day and hunt at dusk and at night. Average life expectancy for Mexican spotted owls in the wild is about 15 years. Learn more>

Peregrine Falcon

The open landscape and high cliffs of Zion are perfect habitat for peregrine falcons. Crevices found in the sheer rock faces within the park, including those on Angels Landing, serve as nesting locations for peregrines to rear their chicks. Climbing routes on any cliffs used by nesting peregrines temporarily close every year from March 1 to mid- to late-summer in order to protect the birds and their young. Learn more>
