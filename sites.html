Sites to Visit

Angels Landing

Angels Landing is one of the most popular destinations in Zion National Park. Many who go there want to experience untamed adventure and get a classic photograph. Its' now famous name descends from Methodist minister Frederick Vining Fisher who, on his first visit to Zion Canyon in 1916, allegedly quipped "only an angel could land there." Learn more>

Canyon Overlook Trail

This short, scenic trail brings you to a high overlook above the Great Arch and provides panoramic vistas of Zion Canyon's lower reaches. It features sandstone steps, a metal handrail, shaded alcoves, a narrow bridge, and a fenced cliff edge. Learn more>

Court of the Patriarchs

Enjoy spectacular views of the three patriarchs: Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob. The three peaks were named in 1916 by Methodist minister Frederick Vining Fischer and two local boys from Southern Utah. The three peaks are best seen from an overlook on the east side of the Zion Canyon Scenic Drive.

Emerald Pools

The Emerald Pools are a series of pools and waterfalls that can be reached by hiking on a variety of trails. They rank among Zion National Park's most popular and easily accessible natural wonders. The path to the Lower Pool is wide and paved, while the trails leading to the Middle and Upper Emerald Pools are progressively more challenging. Learn more>

The Grotto

The Grotto is a popular trailhead and picnic area with views of the Virgin River and Angels Landing. In the early 1920s, the Civilian Conservation Corps (CCC) built a campground called Grotto at this location, which marked the end of the road into Zion Canyon. The Grotto is located at shuttle stop #6, past Court of the Patriarchs and Zion Lodge.

The Narrows

The Narrows is the narrowest section of Zion Canyon. This gorge, with walls 1,000 feet tall and the river sometimes just 20 to 30 feet wide, is one of the most popular areas in the park. You can see The Narrows by hiking along the paved, wheelchair accessible Riverside Walk for one mile from the Temple of Sinawava. If you wish to see more, you must hike in the Virgin River! Learn more>

Observation Point

Located at the north end of Zion Canyon, 2,100 feet above the canyon floor, Observation Point is a stunning overlook that is south facing and stands 800 feet above Angels Landing highest point in Zion National Park. Panoramic views of The Great White Throne, Cathedral Mountain, Angels Landing, and Cable Mountain can be seen from this vantage point. Learn more>

Zion-Mt. Carmel Tunnel

This 1.1 mile-long tunnel is credited with converting Zion National Park from an isolated and rarely-visited park to one of the most popular sites in the National Park system. It was an engineering marvel when it was completed in the early 1930s. It connects the Lower and Upper Canyons in Zion and is tunneled through solid rock with windows that let in light and allow for airflow. Learn more>
