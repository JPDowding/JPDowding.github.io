General Information

Location & Directions

Zion National Park is located in southern Utah near the city of Springdale, not far from Las Vegas (163 miles), Salt Lake City (307 miles), or St. George (40 miles). The majority of visitors coming from afar fly into either Salt Lake International Airport or Las Vegas International Airport, then rent a car and drive to the park.

Zion Canyon Visitor Center coordinates (near Springdale): 37.200190, -112.987139

Kolob Canyons Visitor Center coordinates (near New Harmony): 37.453462, -113.225640

Operating Hours & Seasons

Zion is open 24 hours a day, 7 days a week, year round. Park visitor center hours change seasonally.

Summer (May-Sep) This is the busiest time to visit Zion, with facility operating hours longest for the year. The park shuttle bus is required.

Fall (Oct-Nov) Shuttle buses are still required but start running a little less frequently and for less hours each day. Visitation begins to slow down.

Winter (Dec-Feb) Typically, park shuttle buses will be used only during the holidays. Access to some roads closes.

Spring (Mar-Apr) The park shuttle returns to full time. Visitation picks up dramically in March during spring break.

Fees & Passes

Everyone who visits Zion needs to have a park pass. Visitors can buy a weekly pass (the cheapest pass available) at the park entrance, or they can show an annual or lifetime pass.

The following (weekly) passes allow access to Zion National Park for seven consecutive days:

Private vehicle: $35

Motorcycle: $30

Person entering by foot or bicycle: $20 (under 15 is free of charge)

Depending on your plans, you may also need to pay a fee for a permit.

Shuttle Schedule & Info

Parking is very limited inside Zion National Park. There are two free shuttles available to visitors:

(1) The Zion Canyon Line, or park shuttle, runs from the Zion Canyon Visitor Center to destinations including the Zion Lodge and trailheads for hikes to Angels Landing, Emerald Pools, West Rim Trail, and The Narrows. Download the Information Guide for a map of the park shuttle route.

(2) The Springdale Line, or town shuttle, provides service in the Town of Springdale. It stops at 9 locations in town and picks up and drops off visitors at the park's pedestrian/bike entrance at Zion Canyon Village.

Lodging

Inside the Park

Zion Lodge - Open year round; hotel rooms and historic cabins.

Watchman Campground - Open year round; tent or RV camping; reservation required.

Lava Point Campground - Open May-Sept; primitive campsites; reservation required.

Outside the Park - Lodging options nearby.

Dining

Inside the Park

Castle Dome Cafe - Seasonal snack bar adjacent to Zion Lodge.

Red Rock Grill - Located in Zion Lodge main building; open for breakfast, lunch, and dinner.
