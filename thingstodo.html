Things to Do

Backpacking

The Zion Wilderness is a world-renowned destination that offers opportunities for solitude and adventure. With over 90 miles of trails, dozens of designated backpacking sites, multiple at-large camping areas, and 124,406 acres of designated wilderness, Zion National Park offers a variety of unique backpacking opportunities. Learn more>

Biking

Bikes are a great way to enjoy Zion National Park and experience the canyon at your own pace. Biking allows you to take in the views, photograph the sights, and observe the abundant wildlife that call Zion home. All park roadways and the Pa'rus Trail are open to bicyclists. Learn more>

Canyoneering

Canyoneering is an outdoor adventure sport that involves exploring canyons using a variety of techniques including hiking, climbing, rappelling, swimming, sliding, jumping, chimneying, and stemming. Zion National Park has become one of the premier places in the country to participate in this exciting activity. Learn more>

Day Hiking

Zion National Park offers a wide range of hiking opportunities, with something suitable for every age and experience level, during every month of the year. The most popular trails are located in Zion Canyon, including Angels Landing, The Narrows, and many others. The most challenging hikes in the park require a permit. Learn more>

Horseback Riding

Guided horseback riding trips are available March through October. Call 1-435-679-8665 or visit www.canyonrides.com, providers of the only ride IN Zion National Park.

River Trips

All watercraft use in the park requires a wilderness permit. There are rules and regulations that you must follow for float trips in Zion. Learn more>

Rock Climbing

Zion's 2,000-foot sandstone cliffs are world renowned for their big wall climbs and adventurous nature. Most routes in the park are not recommended for inexperienced climbers due to difficulty and the soft nature of the rock. Be sure to read about the climbing regulations, seasonal climbing closures, and climbing best practices before attempting to climb in the park. Learn more>

Stargazing

Zion has dark night skies throughout the park. There are some easily accessible night sky viewing areas in Zion Canyon, East Side, Kolob Canyons, and on Kolob Terrace Road. Learn more>
